Lemmy

The godfather of "Dirty" Rock 'n' Roll and Heavy Metal!

Lemmy during Motörhead's 2011 The Wörld Is Yours Tour.

A short timeline of Lemmy's life as musician.

  • 1945 - Born in Stroke-on-Trent, Staffordshire, England.
  • 1960's - Plays in several rock groups such as the Rockin' Vickers. Works as a roadie for Jimi Hendrix and The Nice.
  • 1971 - Joins the space rock band Hawkwind as bassist and a lead vocalist and they record their hit, "Silver Machine".
  • 1975 - He gets fired from Hawkwind after an arrest for illegal drug possession. The same year he founds Motorhead and becomes the lead singer, bassist and songwriter of the band.
  • 1980 - 1981 - Lemmy's band success peaks with several UK chart hits, one of them being the single "Ace of Spades", which remaind crowd favourite throughout the bands career, and the UK #1 live album No Sleep 'till Hammersmith.
  • 1981 - 2015 - Lemmy becomes an iconic symbol, and his band "Motorhead" one of the most influential bands in the heavy metal genre.
  • 2015 - Four days after his 70th birthday, Lemmy dies at his apartment in Los Angeles from prostate cancer, cardiac arrhythmia, and congestive heart failure. "Motörhead" announced his death on their official Facebook page later that day. According to the band, his cancer had only been diagnosed two days prior to his death.
